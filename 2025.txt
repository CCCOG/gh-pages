Saturday, December 13 — Psalm 143:1–6
Micah 6,7; Revelation 13:11–14:5

The days are surely coming, says the Lord, when the city shall be rebuilt for the Lord. Jeremiah 31:38

And I saw the holy city, the new Jerusalem, coming down out of heaven from God, prepared as a bride adorned for her husband. And I heard a loud voice from the throne saying, “See, the home of God is among mortals.” Revelation 21:2-3

Gracious God, help us to shape our cities and our neighborhoods by the barometer of Christ’s love. Help us to judge our communities not on the basis of material possessions, but from a place of selfless giving. Amen.


Third Sunday of Advent

Sunday, December 14 — Isaiah 35:1-10; Psalm 146:5-10

The centurion asked Jesus, “But only speak the word, and let my servant be healed.” Luke 7:7

Holy Ghost, help us to listen with the faith of the centurion. May we be reminded of how much you can accomplish with even a faint whisper of compassion. Amen.


Monday, December 15 — Psalm 143:7–12
Nahum 1,2; Revelation 14:6–16

He was wounded for our transgressions, crushed for our iniquities. Isaiah 53:5

God, who did not withhold his own Son but gave him up for all of us, how will he not with him also give us everything else? Romans 8:32

Dear Jesus, may we remember that you were crucified to remove the burden of sin from the world. May our thanks be moved to tears when we see our reflection in your wounds. Amen.


Tuesday, December 16 — Psalm 144:1–4
Nahum 3; Habakkuk 1; Revelation 14:17–15:8

For God alone my soul waits in silence, for my hope is from him. Psalm 62:5

We wait for the blessed hope and the manifestation of the glory of our great God and Savior, Jesus Christ. Titus 2:13

Prince of peace, waiting for you to come this Christmas season is filled with anxiety. Help us to see that waiting can be a productive space to occupy if we use that time to serve the needs of our world. Amen.


Wednesday, December 17 — Psalm 144:5–8
Habakkuk 2,3; Revelation 16:1–11

Woe to those who call evil good and good evil, who put darkness for light and light for darkness. Isaiah 5:20

Do not be overcome by evil, but overcome evil with good. Romans 12:21

Lord of light, help us to stand for justice and righteousness in our world. May our loyalty be to your voice and wisdom and not those who seek power to abuse others. Amen.


Thursday, December 18 — Psalm 144:9–15
Zephaniah 1,2; Revelation 16:12–21

Reform your ways and your actions, and I will let you live in this place. Jeremiah 7:3 NIV

For this very reason, you must make every effort to support your faith with excellence, and excellence with knowledge, and knowledge with self-control, and self-control with endurance, and endurance with godliness, and godliness with mutual affection, and mutual affection with love. 2 Peter 1:5-7

Abba, bless us with strength and courage to change our ways when they block us from loving our Lord and our neighbor. It is never too late to make a positive change for God. Amen.


Friday, December 19 — Psalm 145:1–7

There is no Rock like our God. 1 Samuel 2:2

Be strong in the Lord and in the strength of his power. Ephesians 6:10

Heavenly Father, give us the strength to survive these dark days. Help us to rest our hope on your light which waits for us on the horizon. Amen.


Saturday, December 20 — Psalm 145:8–16
Haggai 2; Revelation 17:9–18

Joseph reassured his brothers, speaking kindly to them. Genesis 50:21

Be kind to one another, tenderhearted, forgiving one another, as God in Christ has forgiven you. Ephesians 4:32

Holy Spirit, help our faith be practical. Equip us to carry out small acts of kindness and forgiveness for our families, friends, and community. Amen.


Fourth Sunday of Advent

Watchword for the week — Restore us, O Lord God of hosts; let your face shine, that we may be saved. Psalm 80:19

Sunday, December 21 — Isaiah 7:10-16; Psalm 80:1-7,10-14
Romans 1:1-7; Matthew 1:18-25

All these things my hand has made, so all these things are mine, says the Lord. But this is the one to whom I will look, to the humble and contrite in spirit who trembles at my word. Isaiah 66:2

When the goodness and loving kindness of God our Savior appeared, he saved us, not because of any works of righteousness that we had done, but according to his mercy. Titus 3:4-5

Merciful Savior, help us to remember that as we wait impatiently for the Christmas gifts we will receive, we already receive the blessed gift of your mercy every single day. Amen.


Monday, December 22 — Psalm 145:17–21

Alas for you who heap up what is not your own! How long will you load yourselves with goods taken in pledge? Habakkuk 2:6

Dear Jesus, help us to be faithful to our Christian witness in our lives and in our world. May we honor the pledges we have made to you. Amen.


Tuesday, December 23 — Psalm 146
Zechariah 3–5; Revelation 18:11–24

Dear Jesus, help us to see that when we sow seeds of faith, hope, and love around us, we can see your reflection in all our labors of love. Amen.
